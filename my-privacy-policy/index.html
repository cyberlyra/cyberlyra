My Privacy Policy

October 3, 2022

I believe we should all write ourselves a privacy policy: a terms of service list against which other companies need to measure up. More about that later. In the meanwhile, here is this site's privacy pledge to you, dear visitor.

Your data is yours. I don't want or need to know you visited my site. I'm not keeping site tracking data. I do not want to authorize any cookies, trackers, browser fingerprinters, or bots. I belive you should be able to your data to yourself, and remain in control of what data you give and to whom. 

I built this site to be as clean as possible. Putting my opt out values first and foremost has buided my decisions about which tools to use and which to lose. You'll notice I didn't set this site up on Substack, Medium, or Wix, or a flashy hosting service that makes it "easy" to blog and subscribe. Instead, I investigated my connections to the tools that help me make it, as deeply as possible.  I don't want to risk your data being sniffed by a third party company when you visit my Opt Out site.

This site is hosted on GitHub. Microsoft owns GitHub.  To the best of my knowledge they do not track people who visit their code repositories, including this site, although they very likely log IP addresses used for access. If you're worried about that, access through a VPN or the Tor browser. GitHub is making privacy policy changes that largely track Enterprise users. If I learn they are tracking site visitors under, I will move this site and update this privacy policy accordingly.

The site is static HTML and CSS. I am using the open source system called Publii to generate the blog, so that the code is as clean as possible (you might also check out the excellent Jekyll and Hugo as blogging options). You can read the source code for my site or for Publii whenever you want to. Note that Publii does use Google Fonts but sources them directly instead of tapping a live Google repository.  (I couldn't really find a solution that didn't have some reliance on a Google product, including the Go language, but at least no Google API's are active when the site loads).

You may subscribe to my mailing list. I chose to go with ButtonDown, because it's run by one guy who is really into data privacy.  You can read their privacy policy here. If you subscribe, ButtonDown will record your email address and keep it on their server.  ButtonDown has promised me that those email addresses belong to me, not to them, and I've opted out of recording anything else about your subscription. If that changes, I'll use my Tech Break Up tool and migrate the newsletter elsewhere.

I will use the email address you give me to send you a newsletter keeping you up to date on posts, tips, how-tos, reviews, and other relevant Opt-Out info. I will never sell this data or use it for anything other than the newsletter. 

In fact, I recommend you sign up with an email alias that redirects to your preferred inbox. The email services offered by Mozilla or DuckDuckGo live in your browser and make it easy. If my mailing list contains nothing but @mozmail and @duck.com aliases, I'll know we're all doing it right!

No trackers. No fingerprints. No data collection. Email aliases whenever possible. This is an Opt Out site, after all. It should be beyond GDPR compliant.

That said, systems change. Also, I am always working to improve. If you notice something tracking you, or that the unique alias you gave to me alone is being used for something you didn't authorize, tell me so I can make the switch to a different system. You can reach me at info (at) optoutproject (dot) net.
