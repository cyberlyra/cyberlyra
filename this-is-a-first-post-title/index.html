    Welcome to the Opt Out Project

    September 13, 2022

    If data is the new oil, I'm here to help you go electric.

    You can live a modern, digitally connected, twenty-first century life online without Big Tech. You don't have to trade your personal data for "free" services to do so. You don't have to use what other people are using. It's not (always) difficult, and you don't have to live in a cave.

    I started opting out in 2012 when I stopped using Google tools and deleted my account. Since then I have built up a treasure trove of knowledge, devices, and services aimed at keeping me and my family away from the evils of data-collecting algorithmic systems.

    Honestly, the last ten years have seen me doing things that most people honestly thought were crazy. Now, they're asking how I do it.

    That's why I started this site. I've decided to put information about these tools and alternatives in one spot for others to find and use. Sure, some of these tools are reviewed in the tech press, and there is information out there if you know where to look. My goal is to bring it all together in one place, to guide you through the process, and help you opt out as well.

    I'm fed up with living in a dystopian version of the future. We should all be able to live our lives without these data-sucking and socially destructive tools torquing our world. 

    I welcome you, fellow and aspiring conscientious objectors!

    You can start here with the Basics, delve into the Techniques I've developed to enable my lifestyle, or explore the Philosophy, the academically-informed thinking behind my unique approach. Or just jump into my reviews of alternative tools and systems: from email and search to cell phones and the devices that power my connected home.  Or maybe you want to know who I am--I don't blame you!--more about that is here or here.

    What this site is NOT

    This site is NOT going to help you with a tech detox. I'm not here to hate on technology, or tell you to go live in a cave. 

    I actually love technology. I'm a sociologist of science and technology and I got a whole PhD in the study of technology and society. I publish research in human-computer interaction and I work with NASA spacecraft teams. I'm now a professor at Princeton, where I am an expert in the study of technology and how it intersects with society. When I'm not teaching or researching I spend my spare time hacking phones and building voice assistants using Raspberry Pi's.

    Of all people, I don't think the answer is to leave technology behind altogether. 

    That said, I think we need to leave these technologies. Google. Meta. Other algorithmic systems that blend search or friendship with advertising, serve information up with a heaping dose of surveillance, and lead us all astray. Companies that went from "Don't be evil" to, well, being evil. Companies that ignored the warning signs and marched us boldly into a world suffused with dysfunction.

    I'm sharing this knowledge because it's time for a better future. It's time to put us all in the drivers' seat, to demand different tools, and to start living a better life online.

    It's time to opt out.
